UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud has become one of the most common types of cybercrime in India, especially among students and people who are new to digital payments. 
A typical case usually starts with a fraudster pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a known contact.
They call or message the victim and create a sense of urgency, such as saying that the bank account will be blocked or a payment is pending.

In one such scenario, a college student received a call from someone claiming to be from the bank. 
The caller asked the student to verify their account by sharing an OTP and clicking on a link.
Trusting the caller, the student followed the instructions without realizing it was a scam.
Within minutes, money was deducted from their account through multiple UPI transactions.

These frauds usually target people who are not fully aware of digital payment security practices.
The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems.

This case highlights how easily people can be manipulated through fear and urgency.
It shows the importance of staying alert while using digital payment platforms and never sharing sensitive information like OTPs or approving unknown payment requests.
